## AI / Machine Learning

### ROCKET — AlphaFold + Experimental Data

**Source:** Nature Methods  

**Abstract**  
ROCKET integrates AlphaFold-style predictions with experimental data such as X-ray crystallography and cryo-EM to improve structural determination. By leveraging learned structural priors, it reduces experimental burden and enhances accuracy. This hybrid approach represents a new paradigm in structural biology workflows. ([link to the publication](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03047-4))

**Keywords:** protein structure, AlphaFold, cryo-EM  

---

### Protein Embedding Uncertainty

**Source:** Nature Methods  

**Abstract**  
This work introduces a framework to quantify uncertainty in protein embeddings, improving reliability in downstream biological predictions. By identifying weak or ambiguous representations, it enhances model robustness and interpretability. This is critical for applications such as function prediction and variant analysis. ([link to the publication](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03028-7))

**Keywords:** embeddings, uncertainty, protein modeling

### Protein Language Model Distillation

**Source:** Nature Methods  

**Abstract**  
This work demonstrates how co-distillation can compress multiple protein language models into a single high-performance model for variant effect prediction. The approach eliminates reliance on structural or genetic data while maintaining accuracy. It significantly broadens accessibility of advanced protein modeling. ([link to the publication](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03050-9))

**Keywords:** protein language models, variant prediction  

---

### VESM — Sequence-Only PLM

**Source:** Nature Methods  

**Abstract**  
VESM aggregates predictions from multiple protein language models into a single sequence-only model that outperforms hybrid approaches. It also provides quantitative severity scores for variants, improving interpretability. This enhances both clinical and research applications in genomics. ([link to the publication](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03049-2))

**Keywords:** PLM, genomics, variant severity  

---

### CLOP-DiT — Generative Single-Cell Models

**Source:** bioRxiv  

**Abstract**  
CLOP-DiT enables generation of realistic single-cell RNA-seq data from structured biological descriptions using a diffusion-based architecture. It aligns text and cellular embeddings to produce controllable gene expression outputs. This opens new possibilities for simulation, data augmentation, and hypothesis testing in single-cell biology. ([link to the publication](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.26.714457v1))

**Keywords:** single-cell, generative models, diffusion  

---

### OPTIMIS — AI-Controlled Therapy Simulation

**Source:** bioRxiv  

**Abstract**  
OPTIMIS combines stochastic biological modeling with neural ODEs and reinforcement learning to simulate and optimize therapeutic interventions. It enables adaptive, closed-loop treatment strategies that respond to dynamic biological signals. This framework represents a step toward personalized, AI-driven medicine. ([link to the publication](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.24.713941v1))

**Keywords:** reinforcement learning, therapy optimization, simulation  

---

### FoundedPBI — Phage-Bacterium Prediction

**Source:** bioRxiv  

**Abstract**  
FoundedPBI uses genomic foundation models and ensemble learning to predict phage-bacterium interactions with high accuracy. By leveraging long-context genomic data, it improves prediction performance over existing methods. This approach accelerates development of phage therapy as an alternative to antibiotics. ([link to the publication](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.24.713871v1))

**Keywords:** phage therapy, genomics, foundation models
