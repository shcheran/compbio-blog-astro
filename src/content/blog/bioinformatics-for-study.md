# Scientific Research & Bioinformatics Resources Digest

This collection is a hands-on learning resource for students and researchers looking to build practical skills in bioinformatics, computational biology, and data analysis. It combines key research papers with tools, tutorials, and curated resources to help you both understand core concepts and apply them in real workflows.

The focus is on **learning by doing** — exploring biological systems, working with real data, and using modern software to perform analyses. Whether you're starting out or advancing your skills, this set of articles provides a practical pathway to develop applied, job-relevant expertise.

## Research & Studies

### Lagging Strand DNA Replication & Stress Response

**Source:** PubMed (Cell Cycle)

**Summary**  
This study presents an updated model of lagging-strand DNA replication, showing how the replication stress response monitors Okazaki fragment synthesis to regulate replication dynamics. It reveals how cells coordinate origin firing and replication progression to maintain genomic stability under stress conditions. These findings deepen our understanding of replication fidelity and genome maintenance mechanisms. ([link to the publication](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41865289/))

**Keywords:** DNA replication, replication stress, Okazaki fragments, genome stability  

---

### Vaccine Hesitancy & COVID-19 Interaction

**Source:** PubMed (Human Vaccines & Immunotherapeutics)

**Summary**  
This large-scale study of university students demonstrates how vaccine hesitancy interacts dynamically with personal COVID-19 experiences and beliefs to influence booster uptake. It highlights the importance of context-specific behavioral factors and evolving perceptions in shaping vaccination decisions. The results emphasize the need for targeted public health interventions to improve vaccine adoption. ([link to the publication](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41639035/))

**Keywords:** vaccine hesitancy, COVID-19, public health, behavior, immunology  

---

### Preoperative Planning Tool for Thoracotomy

**Source:** PubMed (Computer Assisted Surgery)

**Summary**  
This work introduces a web-based, patient-specific planning tool that uses ray-casting techniques to optimize surgical incision placement in pediatric cardiac procedures. By quantifying anatomical visibility and access, it improves surgical precision and reduces operative risks. The approach enhances both safety and cosmetic outcomes in complex surgeries. ([link to the publication](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41793778/))

**Keywords:** surgical planning, thoracotomy, medical imaging, computational modeling  

---

### Actin Network Regulation in C. elegans

**Source:** bioRxiv

**Summary**  
This study shows how capping protein regulates competition between actin networks by controlling filament growth at barbed ends in C. elegans zygotes. It demonstrates how limited actin resources are allocated across competing structures, influencing cell morphology and dynamics. These insights advance our understanding of cytoskeletal organization during early development. ([link to the publication](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.23.713757v1))

**Keywords:** actin, cytoskeleton, cell dynamics, development, C. elegans  

---

### Hippocampal Spatial Coding via CA1 Inputs

**Source:** bioRxiv

**Summary**  
This research reveals that topographic projections from CA1 neurons shape spatial representations in the subiculum without altering individual neuron tuning. It highlights how anatomical wiring contributes directly to functional coding and network stability. These findings provide new insights into how spatial memory and navigation are organized in the brain. ([link to the publication](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.24.714092v1))

**Keywords:** neuroscience, hippocampus, spatial coding, neural circuits  

---

### Condensin Complexes in Chromatin Organization

**Source:** PubMed (Nucleus)

**Summary**  
This review explores how condensin complexes organize chromosomes through ATP-dependent loop extrusion, shaping chromatin architecture during cell division. It also connects condensin dysfunction to diseases such as cancer and neurodevelopmental disorders. The work provides a comprehensive framework linking chromosome structure to disease mechanisms. ([link to the publication](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41664839/))

**Keywords:** chromatin, condensin, chromosome structure, cancer, genetics

## Final Takeaways

Recent research continues to deepen our understanding of fundamental biological systems, from DNA replication and cytoskeletal dynamics to neural coding and chromatin organization.
